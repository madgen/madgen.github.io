This is Mistral Contrastin's personal website. I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Computer Science and Technology, the University of Cambridge. Here you can find some information about my research, teaching as well as technical and cultural rumblings.

My research is in programming languages and verification. I worked on static analysis of Fortran (chill, using Haskell). I am now working on a Datalog variant for querying data under version control. Also I am an ex-Rubyist, up-and-coming Haskeller, and wannabe Agda programmer.

If I am not hacking in the nearest hip café or chasing the hottest art films I barely understand, I can be found running mid-distance by the River Cam.

I have a Twitter account and I prefer mentions over emails.
